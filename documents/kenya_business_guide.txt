KENYA BUSINESS REGISTRATION & FINANCING GUIDE
For Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs)

=== BUSINESS REGISTRATION IN KENYA ===

HOW TO REGISTER A BUSINESS IN KENYA

Step 1: Choose Your Business Structure
- Sole Proprietorship: One owner, personal liability, simplest and cheapest to register
- Partnership: Two or more people, shared profits and liability
- Limited Liability Company (LLC): Separate legal entity, owners protected from business debts
- Co-operative Society: Group of people pooling resources together

Step 2: Register with the Business Registration Service (BRS)
- Website: https://brs.go.ke or eCitizen portal: https://ecitizen.go.ke
- Sole proprietorship costs approximately KES 950
- Limited company registration costs approximately KES 10,650
- Processing time: 1-3 business days online

Step 3: Get a Business Permit from your County Government
- Required for operating in a specific location
- Cost varies by county and business type (typically KES 5,000-50,000 per year)
- Nairobi City County permits: https://nairobi.go.ke

Step 4: Register with KRA for a PIN
- Every business MUST have a KRA Personal Identification Number (PIN)
- Register for free at: https://itax.kra.go.ke
- Required for: filing taxes, opening bank accounts, applying for government tenders

=== TAX OBLIGATIONS FOR KENYAN MSMEs ===

1. Turnover Tax (TOT)
- For businesses with annual turnover between KES 1 million and KES 50 million
- Rate: 3% of gross monthly sales
- Filed monthly by the 20th of the following month
- Replaces income tax for eligible small businesses

2. Value Added Tax (VAT)
- Mandatory registration if annual turnover exceeds KES 5 million
- VAT rate: 16% on standard-rated goods and services
- Some goods are zero-rated (basic foodstuffs, exported goods)
- VAT returns filed monthly by the 20th of the following month

3. Pay As You Earn (PAYE)
- Required if you have employees on payroll
- Deducted from employee salaries and remitted to KRA
- Filed and paid monthly by the 9th of the following month
- PAYE bands: 0-24,000 KES (10%), 24,001-32,333 KES (25%), above 32,333 KES (30%)

4. Income Tax
- Annual income tax returns filed by 30th June each year
- Corporate tax rate: 30% for resident companies
- Individual business owners pay personal income tax on business profits

TAX PENALTIES
- Late filing penalty: 5% of tax due or KES 2,000, whichever is higher
- Late payment: 5% of unpaid tax plus 1% interest per month
- KRA can audit businesses up to 7 years back — always keep proper financial records

=== FINANCING OPTIONS FOR KENYAN MSMEs ===

GOVERNMENT FINANCING PROGRAMS

1. Hustler Fund (Financial Inclusion Fund)
- Launched November 2022 by the Government of Kenya
- Access via M-PESA by dialing *254#
- Personal loan amounts start from KES 500
- Business loan amounts up to KES 50,000
- Interest rate: 8% per annum
- Eligibility: Kenyan citizen with national ID and active M-PESA account
- 5% of each loan automatically goes to savings

2. Youth Enterprise Development Fund (YEDF)
- Target: Kenyan youth aged 18-35 years
- Loan amounts: KES 50,000 to KES 500,000
- Interest rate: 8% per annum
- Apply at: yedf.go.ke or nearest Huduma Centre
- Requirements: Business plan, national ID, passport photos

3. Women Enterprise Fund (WEF)
- Target: Women-owned and women-led businesses
- Loan amounts: KES 50,000 to KES 500,000
- Interest rate: 8% per annum
- Apply through registered women groups or wef.go.ke
- Repayment period: Up to 3 years

4. Uwezo Fund
- Target: Women, youth and persons with disabilities
- Loans disbursed through registered self-help groups
- Amounts: Up to KES 500,000 per group

SACCO FINANCING
- SACCOs offer loans at lower interest rates than commercial banks
- Typical rate: 12-14% per annum (reducing balance)
- Must be a member with savings history (usually 3-6 months)
- Loan amounts typically 3x your savings
- Examples: Mwalimu National SACCO, Harambee SACCO, Tower SACCO, Stima SACCO

COMMERCIAL BANK MSME PRODUCTS
- KCB Bank: KCB M-PESA loans, SME working capital loans
- Equity Bank: Equitel loans, dedicated SME banking products
- Co-operative Bank: MCo-op Cash mobile loans, co-op business loans
- NCBA Bank: Loop digital loans for small businesses
- Absa Bank: SME business loans and overdraft facilities

MICROFINANCE INSTITUTIONS
- Kenya Women Microfinance Bank (KWFT): Focuses on women entrepreneurs
- Faulu Microfinance Bank: Business loans for small enterprises
- SMEP Microfinance Bank: Credit for micro and small businesses

=== LICENSES AND COMPLIANCE ===

1. Single Business Permit (County Government)
- Required for ANY business operating within a county
- Renewed annually between January and March
- Failure to have one can result in business closure

2. Food Handling Certificate
- Required for all businesses handling food (restaurants, kiosks, food processing)
- Issued by the county public health department

3. KEBS Standards Mark
- Required for manufacturers and importers
- Kenya Bureau of Standards: kebs.org

4. Fire Safety Certificate
- Required for businesses with premises
- Issued by county fire departments after inspection

IMPORTANT COMPLIANCE REMINDERS
- Always renew business permits on time to avoid penalties
- Keep all financial records for at least 7 years for KRA purposes
- Register employees for NSSF and NHIF
- Display your business permit and licenses visibly at your premises
