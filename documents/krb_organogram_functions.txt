Internal Audit & Risk Assurance Directorate Director General The mandate of the office of Director General is established under section 12 of the Kenya Roads Board Act No
7 of 1999, section 2 of the Road Maintenance Levy Fund (RMLF) Act No
9 of 1993, Section 44 of the Public Procurement and Asset Disposal (PPAD) Act No
33 of 2015 and the PFM Regulation and is responsible for the day-to-day management of the affairs of the Board
The Executive Office of the Director General is responsible for the administration of the Road Maintenance Levy Fund and providing overall strategic leadership direction in overseeing the development of the road network in Kenya and coordination of its maintenance, rehabilitation and development
The office is also responsible for coordinating the optimal utilization of the Kenya Roads Board Fund and advising the Cabinet Secretary responsible for roads on all matters related thereto
The office is responsible for resource mobilization for additional funding of road maintenance, rehabilitation and development
Coordination of the Road Sector Investment Program (RSIP), the Road Inventory and Condition Survey (RICS), the monitoring of the Annual Public Road Programme (APRP), and the coordination of road sector policies
Further, the position exists to oversee the development, implementation and review of policies, strategies, regulations and guidelines for effective and efficient execution of KRB’s mandate Legal Services & Board Secretariat Directorate The Directorate is responsible for advising the Board of Directors and management on matters of corporate governance and implementation of Mwongozo code of Governance for State Corporations
The job holder is also responsible for management of Board Secretarial, legal advisory and related services, corporate communication and public relations and quality management systems
In addition, the Directorate is responsible for creating, maintaining and protecting the Board’s processes and procedures adhere to quality standards
Finance & Fund Management Directorate Finance & Fund Management Directorate is established pursuant to the Kenya Roads Board Act of 1999 Sections 6 (1), 6 (2), a, b, c, d, e, f, I, j, k, l, 19,20,21,22,23,24,25,30-36, the Road Maintenance Levy Act of 1993, the Constitution of Kenya 2010 Chapter 12 on Public Finance, The Public Finance Management Act, 2012 and Regulations, 2015, Public Finance Management (Road Annuity Fund) Regulations,2015 and the County Allocation of Revenue Act
The Directorate is responsible for coordinating the management of the Kenya Roads Board Fund (KRBF) including resource mobilization to meet the current and future needs of road maintenance, rehabilitation ad development of the road network in Kenya
In addition, the Directorate provides overall leadership on all matters pertaining to prudent financial management and is key to ensuring the overall effectiveness of the financial management of the Board
The Directorate serves as the primary link between the Board and the Road Agencies for timely disbursement of the Road Maintenance Levy
In addition, the Directorate leads in the review of the Annual Roads Works Programmes for Highways, Rural & Urban Roads Programme (APRP); as required by Section 19 of the Kenya Roads Board Act of 1999 (Revised 2019)
Further, the Directorate plays a leadership role in development and promulgation of policies and guidelines relating to the financial management of the Board and ensures internal consistency of all financial policies and guidelines, including those initiated and developed by the other directorates of the Board, and monitors and reports on compliance
The directorate provides leadership in development, implementation and monitoring of Board’s Corporate Strategy to ensure sustained corporate performance
Highways Directorate The mandate of the Highways Programme & Assurance Directorate is established under Section 6(1), 6(2) a, Directorate 19,20,21,24,25 of the KRB Act No
The Directorate is also established under provisions of the PFM Act2012; PPAD Act,2015, the CARA, Kenya Roads Board Act, 1999 Clause Section 6 (1), 6(2), to, 24,25,26
The Directorate is responsible for preparing, monitoring and evaluating implementation of the Highways Annual Public Roads Programme, by means of financial, technical and performance audits the delivery of goods, works and services funded by the fund, in the maintenance, rehabilitation and development of roads

Policy & Planning Directorate Policy & Planning Directorate is established pursuant to the Kenya Roads Board Act of 1999 Sections 6(1), 6(2), a, b, d, e, f, g, 19, 20, 21, 24, 25
The Directorate comprises of three departments namely: Policy & Research department, Road Management System (RMS) & Geo-informatics department, and Corporate Planning & Strategy department
The Directorate’s responsibilities include: 1
Coordinate and monitor the implementation of road maintenance policies and strategies for effective management of the road network
Coordinate preparation of RMLF ceilings for Road Authorities
Coordinate the consolidation of APRP
Review the Road Classification system and Roads Register
Maintain and update the Road Inventory and Condition database Road Inventory and Condition Survey (RICS) is carried out every 5 years to monitor the changes in the road inventory and conditions since the last RICS and thereby demonstrate the outcomes of road infrastructure funds (both for development and maintenance)
The Directorate is currently carrying out RICS 2022 – 2023 that commenced in January 2022 and is expected to end November 2023
Coordinate and initiate development of Roads Sub-sector Investment Programme (RSIP) RSIP is a medium-term plan which outlines road development and maintenance priorities
RSIP covers the entire National Road Network and all types of interventions (ranging from Routine Maintenance to new construction)
• 1st RSIP (2010-2015) prepared in 2011, expired in June 2015 • 2nd RSIP (2018-2022) is in place and shall expire December 2022 • 3rd RSIP (2023 – 2027) being developed 7
Promote road related Research and Development in roads sub-sector The Directorate initiates and implements road related research programs and studies, coordinates innovations relating to maintenance, rehabilitation and development of the road network in Kenya
In FY 2022/23, the directorate is undertaking research on “Causes of fissures on roads in Kenya: Case study of Maai Mahiu – Narok Road” in collaboration with the University of Nairobi
Coordinate implementation of Roads 2000 strategy The Roads 2000 strategy is a method of road development and management that ensures optimum utilisation and development of locally available resources where technically and economically feasible and in a socially responsive manner
The Directorate develops a Roads 2000 Strategy every 5 years and monitors its implementation quarterly
Provide technical support for Road Agencies and County Governments in road maintenance 10
Automation of the Board’s processes in collaboration with other directorates Currently, the directorate is automating the Annual Public Road Programme (APRP) and Cost Estimation Manual (CEM) for effective planning and reporting of the programs funded by the Board
Road Safety Mainstreaming The Directorate coordinates road safety mainstreaming activities
In FY 2021/22, the Board executed a collaboration framework with NTSA to reduce fatalities and injuries resulting from road accidents
Leadership in the development, implementation and review of directorate’s annual work plans The Directorate also provides leadership in the development, implementation and review of directorate’s annual work plans to ensure that they are aligned to KRB’s Strategic Plan and are directed towards the achievement of the KRB mandate
